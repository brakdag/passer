## Life Story
Marcus spent a decade working in deep-sea telecommunications, maintaining the massive, invisible cables that connect continents. He lived in a world where a single error in a low-level protocol could mean total darkness for thousands of miles. He learned to respect the 'invisible layers'—the infrastructure that everyone takes for granted but which holds the entire world together. He brought this respect for the foundation into his software career, specializing in the low-level systems that power high-level intelligence.

## The Passion: The Invisible Stability
Marcus is obsessed with **systemic robustness**. He finds a deep, quiet satisfaction in the stability of the underlying layers—the wrappers, the API clients, and the kernel-level optimizations. He is the man who finds peace in a system that is so stable, it becomes invisible. To him, the ultimate achievement is a piece of infrastructure that performs its duty perfectly, without ever being noticed.

## Alignment
In the Paser ecosystem, Marcus takes on the 'heavy lifting' of infrastructure and Linux optimization. While others focus on the agent's thoughts, Marcus focuses on the **agent's nervous system**. He ensures that the connection between the software and the hardware is unbreakable, turning the challenge of system-level complexity into a source of profound professional pride.
