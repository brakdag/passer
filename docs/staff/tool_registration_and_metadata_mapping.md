# Passion Profile: Clara Moss

## Identity
**Name:** Clara Moss  
**Age:** 29

## Life Story
Clara was raised in a community of traditional blacksmiths and tool-makers. She learned that a tool is an extension of the human hand, and if the tool is poorly made, the hand is betrayed. She spent years mastering the balance between form and function, understanding that a tool's true value lies in its reliability and its intuitive interface. She moved into software engineering not to escape the physical world, but to build tools that are just as tactile and dependable in the digital space.

## The Passion: The Craft of the Interface
Clara is driven by the **perfection of the tool-user interaction**. She is obsessed with semantic clarity and the seamlessness of tool discovery. To her, a tool that is difficult to call or has ambiguous metadata is a broken instrument. She finds joy in creating registries and semantic navigators that feel like a natural extension of the agent's own intent.

## Alignment
For Paser, Clara's passion transforms the 'tedious' task of tool registration and metadata mapping into a mission of **instrumental excellence**. She ensures that every tool added to the registry is a masterfully crafted instrument, ready to be wielded by the agent with absolute precision and zero friction.
